About the Program

Apollo Mentors Project

A student-led nonprofit connecting young musicians with high school mentors who inspire, encourage, and grow alongside them.

Our Story

The Apollo Mentors Project (AMP) was founded by Samuel Elkhunovich, a sophomore at Harvard-Westlake School, with a simple but powerful belief: music is more meaningful when it's shared. Samuel noticed that many younger students struggle with the monotony of solo practice ‚Äî and that high school musicians have so much to offer as role models and practice partners.

AMP was established as an independent nonprofit dedicated to bringing this vision to life across Los Angeles.

üè´   Most mentors are Harvard-Westlake high school students, with a loose affiliation to the school.

What We Do

We match children in grades 3‚Äì5 who play a musical instrument with a high school mentor. Pairs meet once a week for one-hour sessions to practice together, explore music, and build a lasting mentorship relationship.

Sessions are scheduled at mutually convenient times ‚Äî weekdays 3‚Äì7 PM or weekends 9 AM‚Äì7 PM. We work carefully to find the right match based on instrument, neighborhood, and availability.

Who We Serve

Our mentees are young musicians from elementary schools across the Los Angeles area. While not a primary focus, AMP is committed to making the program accessible to students from all backgrounds and communities, and the program is entirely free for all mentees.

Mentors are primarily high school students from Harvard-Westlake School, though motivated students from other high schools are also welcome to apply.

Why It Works

For a young child, practicing scales alone is far less motivating than working alongside someone they look up to. AMP mentors aren't just practice partners ‚Äî they're role models who show mentees what dedication to music looks like.

For mentors, the program builds genuine leadership and communication skills while giving them the rewarding experience of making a real difference in a younger student's life.

Get Involved

Whether you're a student, parent, educator, or community member, there's a way to be part of AMP. Students in grades 3‚Äì5 can sign up as mentees. High school musicians can apply to be mentors. Teachers and school counselors can refer students directly.
